In my computer science major, I have developed a broad and coherent body of knowledge in computer science and learned to apply an integrated understanding of these concepts to solve relevant problems. I have learned to construct models of computational processes and apply key ideas from the theory of computation and its limits. Furthermore, I have learned to design, construct, and explain efficient solutions to a wide range of computational tasks, both by applying known data structures and algorithms, including those found in the literature of the field, and by designing new algorithms using a range of algorithm design techniques to produce runnable implementations of these solutions. Importantly, I can also evaluate the correctness and efficiency of algorithms, both standard and novel, and communicate the evaluation effectively. Finally, I am able to design and code programs with clients to achieve an efficient computational solution to a task, working individually and as part of collaborative teams. I know how to make such solutions work with the capabilities of the hardware and software stack, and understand and explain to others how the underlying infrastructure affects application performance.
